🧠 1. Basic Concept of Percentage

A percentage (%) literally means “per hundred.” It is expressed as: $$X\% = \frac{X}{100}$$ When you calculate X% of Y, you are determining: $$\text{Portion} = \frac{X}{100} \cdot Y$$

🔢 2. The Percentage Formula

$$\boxed{X\% \text{ of } Y = \frac{X \cdot Y}{100}}$$

This calculation is the foundation of most finance and business metrics.

✏️ 3. Step-by-Step Method

To find 20% of 150, follow these steps:

  1. Step 1: Convert to fraction: $$20\% = \frac{20}{100} = 0.20$$
  2. Step 2: Multiply: $$0.20 \times 150$$
  3. Step 3: Result: $$30$$

⚡ 4. Shortcut Method

Apply decimal conversion directly: $$10\% = 0.1, \quad 25\% = 0.25, \quad 50\% = 0.5$$

📊 5. Linear Relationship

The relationship follows $$y = mx$$, where $$m = \frac{Y}{100}$$. This creates a straight line graph.

🧮 6. Real-Life Examples

💰 Discounts: 30% off on ₹1000

$$\text{Discount} = \frac{30 \times 1000}{100} = 300$$ Final Price: $${1000 - 300} = {700}$$

📚 Marks: 80% of 50 marks

$$\text{Marks Obtained} = \frac{80}{100} \times 50 = 40$$

🛒 GST: 18% tax on ₹500

$$\text{Tax} = \frac{18 \times 500}{100} = 90$$ Total Payable: $${500 + 90 = 590}$$

🧩 7. Conclusion

Calculating X% of Y is a fundamental multiplication problem. Whether in academics, statistics, or daily commerce, understanding this linear relationship ensures accurate results every time.

🧠 1. Core Logic of Proportions

Asking "X is what percent of Y" is the same as asking what fraction of the whole ($Y$) the part ($X$) represents, scaled to a base of 100. $$\text{Decimal Value} = \frac{X}{Y}$$ To convert this decimal into a percentage, we multiply by 100.

🔢 2. The Percentage Formula

$$\boxed{P\% = \left( \frac{X}{Y} \right) \times 100}$$

In this finance-ready formula, $X$ is the "Part" and $Y$ is the "Whole."

✏️ 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

Problem: 40 is what percent of 200?

  1. Step 1: Divide the part by the whole: $$\frac{40}{200} = 0.2$$
  2. Step 2: Multiply by 100: $$0.2 \times 100$$
  3. Step 3: Add the % symbol: $$20\%$$

⚡ 4. Mental Math Shortcut

Divide both numbers by 10 or 100 first to simplify the fraction: $$\frac{40}{200} \rightarrow \frac{4}{20} \rightarrow \frac{1}{5} = 20\%$$

📊 5. The Ratio Perspective

This calculation represents the slope in a linear system where $X$ is a subset of $Y$. It defines the relative weight of a metric.

🧮 6. Real-World Applications

📈 Growth: ₹200 profit on ₹1000 sales

$$\text{Profit Margin} = \frac{200}{1000} \times 100 = 20\%$$

🎓 Exam Efficiency: 45 marks out of 60

$$\text{Score Percentage} = \frac{45}{60} \times 100 = 75\%$$

🧩 7. Summary

Determining X is what percent of Y is the standard way to measure efficiency, progress, and composition in data science and commerce.
