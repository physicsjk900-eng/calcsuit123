The Standard Formula
$$EMI = [P \times R \times (1+R)^n] \div [(1+R)^n – 1]$$

Breaking Down the Components

Principal (P)

The raw amount you borrow before interest is added. Lowering your principal with a down payment significantly reduces total interest.

Rate (R)

The cost of borrowing, usually expressed as an Annual Percentage Rate (APR). Even a 0.5% difference can save thousands over long tenures.

Tenure (n)

The duration of the loan. While longer tenures lower your monthly EMI, they drastically increase the total interest you pay over time.

Pro Strategy: The 20/4/10 Rule

Financial experts recommend this rule for car buying: Put down 20%, limit the tenure to 4 years, and keep your total monthly transportation costs below 10% of your gross income.

  • Reduces risk of "underwater" loans
  • Minimizes total interest wastage
  • Protects your monthly cash flow

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pay off my loan early?

Most modern loans allow prepayment, but some include "prepayment penalties." Check your loan agreement before making large extra payments.

Fixed vs. Floating Rates?

Fixed rates stay the same throughout the tenure, offering stability. Floating rates change with market conditions, which can be cheaper or more expensive over time.
