Financial Study Guide

Understand the mechanics of Equated Monthly Installments.

Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed payment made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs apply to both interest and principal each month, so over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

$$E = P \times r \times \frac{(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1}$$

Where E is EMI, P is Principal Loan Amount, r is monthly interest rate, and n is duration in months.

  • Fixed Interest: The rate stays the exact same for the entire loan duration. Highly predictable, but usually starts higher than floating rates.
  • Floating Interest: The rate is tied to an index or base rate. It can fluctuate, meaning your EMI (or loan tenure) will change over time depending on the broader economy.
