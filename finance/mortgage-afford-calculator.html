The 28/36 Comparison

Lenders typically follow the 28/36 rule: Your monthly housing expense (PITI) shouldn't exceed 28% of gross income, and total debt shouldn't exceed 36%. Our calculator applies this rule automatically to keep you in the "safe zone."

Debt-to-Income (DTI)

Your DTI ratio is the percentage of your gross monthly income used to pay debts. A lower DTI indicates a more stable borrower and often qualifies you for lower interest rates and better loan terms.

Affordability Intelligence

Can I afford more if I have a higher credit score?

Technically yes. A higher credit score usually qualifies you for a lower interest rate, which reduces your monthly payment and allows you to borrow more principal for the same monthly cost.

What isn't included in this calculation?

Our basic tool focuses on Principal & Interest (P&I). In the real world, you also need to account for property taxes, homeowners insurance, and HOA fees, which can add 20-30% to your monthly payment.
