Health Study Guide

Master the concepts behind the Body Mass Index.

The Body Mass Index (BMI) maps your weight to your height. It's a widely used clinical screening tool to roughly categorize an adult into underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese ranges.

The formula in imperial units is: [Weight (lbs) / Height (in)²] × 703.

While extremely useful for population studies and general screening, BMI is NOT a diagnostic tool for individual health. Why? Because it completely ignores Body Composition (muscle vs. fat).

  • Bodybuilders and athletes often have an "Obese" BMI due to high muscle density.
  • Elderly individuals may have a "Normal" BMI but unhealthy levels of visceral body fat.
