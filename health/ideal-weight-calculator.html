What is my "Ideal" Body Weight?

The term **"Ideal Body Weight" (IBW)** was originally developed for medical use to calculate drug dosages. Today, it serves as a helpful baseline for individuals to understand a healthy weight range based on height, gender, and biological skeletal frames.

"Remember: IBW is a mathematical estimate. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or overall body composition."

Dual Methodology Accuracy

Our tool cross-references two primary standards to provide the most comprehensive healthy weight profile:

1. The Devine Formula (Scientific Standard)

The clinical standard for estimating weight based on inches over 5 feet. It is the most widely cited formula in medical literature.

2. Healthy BMI Calibration (World Health Std)

While the Devine formula gives a single point, we also provide the 18.5 - 25.0 BMI range, which represents the weight spectrum associated with the lowest long-term health risks.

Important Considerations

When reviewing your results, keep these factors in mind:

  • Athletes: If you have high muscle mass, you may safely weigh more than your "Ideal" weight.
  • Frame Size: People with larger bone structures (large frames) naturally weigh more than those with small frames at the same height.
  • Age: As we age, a slightly higher BMI target is often associated with better health outcomes and bone density support.
