15%

The Standard Baseline

In many regions, 15% is considered the baseline for good service. If you're unsure where to start, this percentage ensures the staff is fairly compensated for a standard dining experience.

20%

The Excellence Tier

Outstanding service often warrants a 20-25% tip. This is common in high-end establishments or when a server goes above and beyond to personalize your visit.

Etiquette
Protocol

International standards vary. In some cultures, tipping is expected, while in others, it may be perceived as rude.

North America

Mandatory for service workers; generally 18-22% is expected in restaurants.

Europe / UK

Often included as a "Service Charge." Otherwise, 10-12% is a standard polite gesture.

Japan / Korea

Rarely required and sometimes declined. Exceptional service is usually included in the price.

Australia / NZ

Not mandatory but appreciated for exceptional hospitality. Generally 10%.
