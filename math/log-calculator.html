What Is a Logarithm?

A logarithm is the inverse operation of exponentiation. In other words, if $$b^y = x$$, then:

$$\log_b(x) = y$$

Where:

  • b is the base ($$b > 0, b \neq 1$$)
  • x is the number you want to find the logarithm of
  • y is the exponent

Examples:

  • $$\log_2(8) = 3$$, because $$2^3 = 8$$
  • $$\log_{10}(1000) = 3$$, because $$10^3 = 1000$$
  • $$\ln(e^5) = 5$$, where ln is the natural logarithm (base e)
