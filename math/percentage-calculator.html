Mastering the Percent

"Whether you're shopping in London or analyzing stocks in New York, percentages are the universal language of change. A common mistake is calculating percentage increase vs. decrease—decreasing a value by 50% and then increasing it by 50% does NOT get you back to the start. You'd actually need a 100% increase to recover from a 50% drop."

Math Pro Hint

"X% of Y is always equal to Y% of X. Hard to do 8% of 25? Try 25% of 8. It's 2!"

Tip Economy

Standard tipping in the USA is 15-20%. In the EU, it's often 5-10% or just rounding up.

Percentage FAQ

How do I calculate a discount?

Subtract the percentage from 100%, convert it to a decimal, and multiply it by the original price. Or just use our % Discount mode above.

What is percentage change?

Take the (New Value - Old Value) / Old Value. Multiply by 100. This tells you the positive or negative shift relative to the starting point.
