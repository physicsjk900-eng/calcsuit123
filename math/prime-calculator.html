Math Study Guide

Master the concepts behind prime numbers.

A prime number is a whole number greater than 1 whose only divisors are 1 and itself. For example, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, etc.

The number 1 is not considered prime. The number 2 is the ONLY even prime number!

Prime numbers are the fundamental building blocks of modern internet security. Algorithms like RSA rely on the mathematical difficulty of factoring extremely large numbers into their prime components. It's easy for a computer to multiply two massive prime numbers together, but exceptionally difficult to "un-multiply" them back into primes without the key.
