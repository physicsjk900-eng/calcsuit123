What Is Standard Deviation?

Standard deviation (σ) is a key statistical measure that quantifies the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of numbers. A low standard deviation means the values are close to the mean, while a high standard deviation indicates that the data points are spread out over a wider range.

Standard deviation is commonly used in statistics, finance, research analysis, and quality control.
